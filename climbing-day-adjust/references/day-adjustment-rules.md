# Day Adjustment Rules

## Priority Order

1. Protect fingers, shoulders, skin, and sleep.
2. Preserve the week's main climbing stimulus.
3. Keep hard days separated.
4. Reduce accessories before removing climbing quality.
5. Log every change so the next weekly plan has context.

## Skip Day

- If Day 1 is skipped, make the next available session the max strength and limit board day only if the athlete feels fresh.
- If Day 2 is skipped, do not cram the whole volume day into Day 3. Add 20 minutes of technique warmup before the hard work instead.
- If Day 3 is skipped, keep the week as two sessions and recover. Do not force a fourth day unless the next week still has spacing.

## Swap Day

- Move the session, not just the label.
- Keep at least one rest or easy day between max strength sessions.
- If two hard sessions become adjacent, make the second session softer or technique-focused.
- If a swap shortens recovery before a project day, cut heavy pulling first.

## Harder Day

Use this only when sleep, skin, fingers, and motivation are good.

Allowed progressions:

- Add 2 to 4 quality attempts on the main project.
- Move from V11 attempts to V12 single moves.
- Add small load to finger strength or weighted pull-ups.
- Extend one link by 1 move.

Do not increase board grade, hang load, and pull-up load in the same session.

## Softer Day

Use this when the athlete still wants to climb but should not push max intensity.

Good substitutions:

- Replace limit board with technique board climbing at 70 to 80 percent.
- Replace max hangs with repeatable submax edge pulls.
- Replace weighted pull-ups with bodyweight pull-ups or rows.
- Use movement drills: quiet feet, hover hands, slow repeats, heel-hook precision.

## Recovery Day

Use this for pain, poor sleep, bad skin, or strong fatigue.

- Full rest is valid.
- Optional: 20 to 30 minutes easy walk, gentle mobility, light shoulder prehab.
- No max hangs, limit board, heavy pulling, or deep lock-off work.

## What To Update In The Journey

- The affected day in `## Current Plan`.
- A dated entry in `## Plan Adjustment Log`.
- The next recommended session.
- Any warning sign to watch before returning to hard climbing.

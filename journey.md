### Week of 2026-06-15 - PIP-Sensitive Capacity Block

**Past week readout**

- Starting baseline is strong: V9 outdoors and V10 on Kilterboard.
- Stronger-climber feedback says to build capacity before max output, and that matches the current symptoms: some sessions feel off and hard Kilter climbing can irritate fingers.
- New finger note: top-of-PIP pain shows up when crimping before warmup and when straightening the finger.
- This week should not be a max-output or full-crimp week. The goal is to leave each session feeling trained, consistent, and finger-safe.
- You will climb Tuesday, Friday, and Sunday.

**Main focus**

- Tuesday 20:00-23:00 is capacity baseline using 5x5-style repeatable board work.
- Friday 17:30-22:00 is volume, technique, and grip-safe movement.
- Sunday 17:30-22:00 is conditional capacity strength plus repeatable links if the PIP joint is calm.
- Keep Wednesday, Thursday, and Saturday as recovery or very light mobility days.
- Treat this block as a test, not a religion. Continue if it improves repeatability, finger tolerance, recovery between attempts, or session quality. Revise if you feel wrecked or climb worse for 2 sessions in a row.
- Hard climbing rule: you can climb hard in body tension, movement complexity, steepness, and coordination. Do not climb hard on finger angle. No full crimp, no thumb-over crimp, no tiny edges, and no painful straightening after the session.

**Day 1 - Tuesday, 2026-06-16, 20:00-23:00 - PIP-safe capacity baseline**

- Morning: 8 to 10 minutes. Do 2 minutes easy breathing or walking, 2 minutes hips, 2 minutes thoracic spine, 2 minutes shoulders and wrists, then check fingers, skin, sleep, and motivation.
- Session warmup: 30 minutes. Easy climbing first, then progressive open-hand loading on friendly holds. Add shoulder activation and 3 to 5 short board moves that feel powerful but controlled. Do not touch small crimps until the finger feels warm and pain-free.
- Grip rule: open hand or relaxed half crimp only. If the PIP joint hurts above 2 out of 10, or if straightening the finger feels worse after warmup, stop board capacity and switch to easy technique climbing.
- Climb: PIP-safe 5x5 board capacity. Pick 5 Kilter or gym boulders around V5 to V7, maybe V8 only if holds are friendly, that you can climb without full crimping. Do up to 5 clean repeats of each. Rest 60 to 120 seconds between repeats and 3 to 5 minutes between problems.
- Hard-ish option: after 45 to 60 minutes, if pain is 0 to 1 out of 10, you may try 2 to 4 harder attempts on open-hand, slopey, pinch, compression, or big-hold problems. No painful crimp attempts.
- Train after board: pull-ups or light weighted pull-ups, 5 sets of 5 at RPE 7. Every rep should be smooth.
- Cooldown: 10 minutes of easy climbing, forearm flush, shoulder mobility, and hip mobility.
- Log: problems chosen, grip types used, clean repeat count, PIP pain 0 to 10 before/during/after, whether straightening hurts after climbing, pull-up load, and whether you felt more consistent by the end.

**Day 2 - Friday, 2026-06-19, 17:30-22:00 - Volume and technique**

- Morning: 8 to 10 minutes. Hips, thoracic spine, shoulders, wrists, and 30 seconds of finger pulses. If fingers still feel loaded from Tuesday, keep the session technical.
- Session warmup: 25 minutes. Easy boulders, foot drills, hip turns, open-hand loading, and 2 to 3 moderate board problems on friendly grips.
- Climb: 60 to 75 minutes of steady volume. Aim for 18 to 26 total boulders from V3 to V7, with most of the work around V5 to V6. No desperate V9/V10 attempts and no full crimp. Prioritize quiet feet, hips close, heel hooks, toe hooks, compression, and tension through foot cuts.
- Hard-ish option: if Tuesday caused no next-morning stiffness and Friday warmup is pain-free, add 3 to 5 attempts on harder open-hand or compression problems. If you need a full crimp to try the move, skip it.
- Train: optional shoulder press or antagonist press, 5 sets of 5 moderate reps. Core for 12 to 15 minutes: toes-to-bar, hollow holds, or front lever progressions at clean form only.
- Cooldown: 10 minutes easy movement plus forearms, lats, and hips.
- Log: total boulders, grades, grip types, how pumped you got, PIP pain 0 to 10, next-morning finger feel, and whether Sunday should stay capacity or go softer.

**Day 3 - Sunday, 2026-06-21, 17:30-22:00 - Capacity strength and repeatable links**

- Morning: 8 to 10 minutes. Breathing, hips, thoracic spine, shoulders, wrists, and a strict readiness check. If the PIP joint is stiff, swollen, or sore when straightening, make this a softer technique day.
- Session warmup: 30 minutes. Easy climbing, progressive board movement, open-hand loading, and 3 short bursts that feel powerful but not desperate.
- Climb: conditional repeatable link capacity. If Tuesday and Friday were calm, choose 2 to 3 climbs around V6 to V8, maybe V9 only if grip-safe, and work links that you can repeat cleanly. Use 4 rounds of 4 links or 5 rounds of 3 links. Rest enough to keep movement crisp, not enough to make it a max session.
- If the PIP joint is not calm: skip links and do 45 to 60 minutes of easy/moderate technique volume on big holds, open hand, slopers, pinches, compression, and feet-first movement.
- Train after board: dips, 5 sets of 5 controlled reps, only if shoulders feel good. Add rows or scap work if fingers need a break.
- Cooldown: easy climbing, forearm recovery, gentle shoulder work, and 5 minutes of hips.
- Log: link count, pump level, PIP pain 0 to 10, whether straightening hurts after, whether any hold type caused pain, and what volume target to repeat next week.

**Recovery and guardrails**

- Stop max finger work immediately if pain feels sharp, nervy, or one-sided.
- No true max hangs or repeated V10+ Kilter limit attempts this week.
- No full crimp, no thumb-over crimp, no pockets, and no small-edge max attempts until the PIP joint is quiet for at least 7 days.
- If fingers are above 3 out of 10 pain during warmup, downgrade to technique climbing and stop board volume early.
- If straightening the finger hurts more after climbing than before climbing, the session was too much.
- If finger soreness carries into the next morning, soften the next climbing day instead of forcing the written plan.
- Change only one variable at a time: repeat count, rest density, grade, or strength load. Do not progress all of them together.
- Keep what works for your actual body response. Drop what only creates soreness, finger flare-ups, or worse movement quality.
- Wednesday: 10 to 20 minutes easy walk plus hips, shoulders, and forearms.
- Thursday: rest or 10 minutes mobility only.
- Saturday: rest, skin care, and light mobility. No hard pulling.
- If skin is the limiter, reduce board attempts before reducing warmup or mobility.
- Progress next week only if finger pain stays 0 to 2 out of 10: add repeats first, then density, then grade.
- Get a climbing-aware hand clinician or PT involved if there is swelling, bruising, a pop, loss of range of motion, daily-life pain, or worsening pain across sessions.
